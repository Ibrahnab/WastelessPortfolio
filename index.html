Wasteless

Meal Planner

A web application design that helps reduce people’s food waste by generating a personalized wasteless meal plan.

About Wasteless

Wasteless is a simple way for busy young adults to plan their meals, keep track of their ingredients and waste less food. With spirit, Wasteless generates a wasteless meal plan based on the users' available ingredients. Wasteless contains a wide selection of recipes, ready in 60 min or less than 60 min. With the recipe directions and ingredients at glance, no more endlessly scrolling to look at how many of the ingredients you need.

The Background

WHY

Students are very busy and occasionally eat alone. This comes with the issue that they have to choose what to eat every day and it is often the case that they have left over ingredients that just expire in the fridge. Food and health are always the topics when it comes to the fundamental needs of people in everyday life. It’s difficult to get a balance between food storing and consumption. One scenario is that one bought some foods from groceries, kept them in the refrigerator, then forgot the existence of some of them. When they found them and realized afterwards, the food had already passed the expiration date and had to end up in the garbage eventually, which is not only unsustainable but also a waste of money.

WHAT

We want to make it easier for people to use all of their food before it goes bad and to provide easy, delicious, quick and cheap recipes that they can plan ahead for the week to prevent food waste. Students usually have a pretty hectic schedule on their hands and deciding on what to cook for the day or the entire week can be time consuming. To support this, an application could be developed that keeps track of what ingredients the user has in their fridge. And make decisions/recommendations on what types of meals can be produced with the available ingredients. For this project we are going to focus on making a website that helps students plan their meals to prevent unnecessary food-waste. Further, the application will hopefully make it easier to plan what meals to cook for the week based on the available ingredients.

Who are the users

The Wasteless web app is dedicated to young adult users who are busy but want to reduce their food waste. The app is meant for those who have trouble choosing what to eat and are looking for easy-to-follow, simple recipes from cuisines all over the world.

THREE DIRECTIONS

PROTOTYPE 1

In this direction, the user starts with inserting which ingredients they have at home, what their food preferences are and what allergies they have.

The app will then generate a meal plan customized to the user’s preferences. The meals will consists of the ingredients that user inserted and possible additional ingredients that the user needs to buy. In the application you have a clear overview that shows what you already have and what you still need to get.Additionally, the user can view and switch recipes to their liking. Thereby creating a meal plan for the entire week and also using resources at home efficiently.

PROTOTYPE 2

In this direction. The user starts off by setting up what meals they’re planning for. The user is then taken to the “Plan your meals” page where they can arrange their meal plan for the day. The application suggests recipes based on the ingredients at home and their expiration date. A “health bar” is shown under the recipe name indicating when it’s about to expire. Additionaly at the top, the application displays total nutritional values from all the meals. The user can add items to their ingredients list by navigating to the “check my ingredients tab”. Similarly, health bars are shown indicating when the item is about to expire. And also showing nutrients in the ingredients per 100 grams This direction therefore allows the user to set up a meal plan for the day and use their resources efficiently while also keeping a close look at their diet.

REFLECTION

The refined purpose of our final prototype was defined as:

“The final prototype has to help doing your part in reducing greenhouse gasses by reducing food waste”

The main feature we developed focuses on creating wasteless meal plans with smooth and intuitive interactions. As our aim was to create a Minimal Viable Product (MVP) and our target group wanted an easy way to choose recipes and reduce waste at the same time, we decided to keep the interaction and features simple. Using a human-centered approach we used our initial user study to empathize with our user group. Based on these we created a persona, Olivia, who represents the target group and helps us imagine how she would use our application. Paired with our own experiences in the kitchen, we created, to the best of our ability, an application that tethers to the needs of our target group.In our process, there are several features we considered adding but eventually decided not to implement including but not limited to having the ability to insert an URL of your own favourite recipes one found online, adding additional ingredients besides the required ingredients for the recipes, a timer, a feature that specifies how to store food properly and a separate feature that suggests what to do with leftover ingredients. The reason why most of these features weren’t implemented is due to either that the feature defies our original purpose of reducing food waste or falls outside of our MVP scope. For future work, the application needs to be evaluated through user testing to see whether this application really has an impact on one's food waste. To summarize, we aimed to create an MVP application, using human-centered design, which helps the users reduce their food waste. We achieved the first two, however, whether this application actually works needs to be further evaluated through user testing.
